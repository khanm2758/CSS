Australian pelican

Appearance:

Australian pelicans are known for their long, pale pink bill, which is the longest of any bird. They have white plumage with black wings and a greyish neck

Habitat:

These pelicans are found in inland and coastal waters of Australia and New Guinea, as well as in Fiji, parts of Indonesia, and occasionally as vagrants in New Zealand

Diet:

They primarily feed on fish, which they catch by using their bill as a scoop. They are also known for their cooperative feeding behavior, where groups of pelicans work together to herd fish into shallow waters for easier capture

Size:

Australian pelicans are large birds, with a wingspan that can reach up to 2.5 meters (8.2 feet)

Behavior:

They are social birds and often seen in groups, especially during feeding
